U.K. Stocks Fluctuate as Mining Shares Rally; Cadbury Declines

Share Business ExchangeTwitterFacebook| Email | Print | A A A

By Sarah Jones

April 9 (Bloomberg) -- The U.K.’s FTSE 100 Index fluctuated as a rally in commodity producers was countered by a sell off in food and beverage companies.

Vedanta Resources Plc climbed more than 7 percent as the company reported record aluminum production and base metals rose amid optimism Chinese government stimulus spending will boost demand. Cadbury Plc and British American Tobacco Plc retreated as Goldman Sachs Group Inc. downgraded their stance on some industries that are less geared to economic growth.

The benchmark FTSE 100 gained 8.36, or 0.2 percent, to 3,933.88 at 11.28 a.m. in London, having dropped as much as 0.3 percent earlier. The index has lost 2.4 percent this week. The broader FTSE All-Share Index rose 0.5 percent today, while Ireland’s ISEQ Index added 0.5 percent. Markets are closed tomorrow for a public holiday on April 10.

“In many ways this market has received some strong ingredients to sustain a decent up move,” said Manus Cranny, senior market commentator at MF Global Spreads in London. We have had a “soupcon of upbeat corporate news and the mining sector has a solid tone with the Vedanta production numbers.”

The FTSE 100 has rebound 12 percent since March 3 as the U.S. Federal Reserve’s announced plans to buy Treasuries and bonds backed by mortgages and Treasury Secretary Timothy Geithner’s pledge to finance the purchase of toxic assets. Japan’s Prime Minister will propose a $154 billion stimulus to help revive the world’s second-largest economy, according to a document obtained by Bloomberg News.

Vedanta Gains

Vedanta climbed 7.8 percent to 833 pence. The company said fourth-quarter output of aluminum gained 31 percent to 134,000 metric tons after the Jharsuguda smelter started operating. Refined zinc output increased 12 percent to 151,000 tons.

Antofagasta Plc, owner of copper mines in Chile, rose 3.9 percent to 548.5 pence. Xstrata Plc, the world fourth- largest copper producer, added 5.8 percent to 554.5 pence.

Copper led base metal price higher as inventories of the metal shrank in London and as Macquarie Securities Ltd. forecast China’s economic growth will be as much as 8 percent this year, 1 percentage point more than its previous estimate. Lead, tin and zinc also increased on the London Metal Exchange.

Cadbury Retreats

Cadbury, the world’s largest confectionary maker, lost 3.6 percent to 516 pence. BAT, Europe’s biggest cigarette maker, lost 2.2 percent to 1,569 pence. Diageo Plc, the world’s largest liquor maker, fell 2.4 percent to 763.5 pence.

Goldman Sachs today said investors in European stocks should increasingly favor industries that rely on economic growth as the global recession eases, downgrading its recommendation for food and beverage makers to “underweight” and on drug shares to “neutral.”

The following stocks also gained or fell in the U.K. market. Stock symbols are in parentheses.

Autonomy Corp. (AU/ LN) increased 23 pence, or 1.7 percent, to 1,358. The U.K.’s second-biggest software maker said earnings and sales probably beat analysts’ estimates as regulation changes and lawsuits lift demand for its database- search software. First-quarter revenue probably reached $128 million to $130 million, more than analyst consensus estimates of $126 million.

Barclays Plc (BARC LN) rallied 10.1 pence, or 6.4 percent, to 167.5 as the U.K.’s third-largest bank nears an agreement to sell its iShares unit to CVC Capital Partners Ltd. for about 3 billion pounds ($4.4 billion), people familiar with the situation said.

Misys Plc (MSY LN) jumped 11.5 pence, or 9.9 percent, to 127.25. The U.K.’s third-biggest software maker said third-quarter revenue rose 69 percent helped by more orders, the strengthening dollar and the merger of its health-care unit with Allscripts Healthcare of the U.S. The company also said it “remains on track to deliver growth and margin targets.”
